Not a model.
Not a survey.
A living twin.

Traditional market research asks humans what they think. Axsona's AI Twins don't answer surveys — they behave. Each twin is a statistically grounded agent instantiated from real census microdata, carrying a full psychological profile, cultural context, economic pressures, media diet, and trust network. When you run a simulation, you're not generating predictions. You're observing emergent behavior at population scale.

Layer 01 // Identity

Census-Calibrated Personas

Every AI Twin is initialized with age, income band, household composition, educational attainment, and geographic context drawn from sovereign census microdata. Demographic fidelity is not estimated — it is sourced. Our calibration corpus spans 14 markets representing 2.4 billion individuals, ensuring each simulated population mirrors the statistical reality of your actual target audience.

Layer 02 // Psychology

Psychographic Deep Structure

Beneath demographics sits a psychographic substrate — values orientation, loss aversion profile, authority trust disposition, and narrative susceptibility index. These dimensions are derived from longitudinal behavioral datasets and validated against real-world decision outcomes. A Twin doesn't just look like your audience. It reasons like them.

Layer 03 // Social Graph

Network Influence Propagation

Humans don't make decisions in isolation. Each Twin is embedded in a synthetic social graph calibrated to real-world network density metrics. When one node shifts, influence propagates. Axsona models cascade effects, herd dynamics, and opinion leader amplification — giving you visibility into the second and third-order consequences of your interventions.

Layer 04 // Temporal

Memory & Belief Drift

Twins remember. Each agent maintains a running belief state that updates as new stimuli are introduced. This allows Axsona to simulate longitudinal campaigns — modeling how brand trust accumulates or erodes over months of exposure, how backlash half-lives, and where narrative fatigue sets in. Real foresight requires modeling time, not just moments.

The anatomy of a single AI Twin

Each agent instantiated in an Axsona simulation carries six discrete data layers. Together, they produce behavior that is statistically indistinguishable from its real-world demographic equivalent.

Stratum I
Demographic Core

Age cohort, income decile, household size, employment sector, and geographic market. Sourced from the most recent available census microdata release for each sovereign territory in our calibration network.

Stratum II
Values Architecture

A 24-dimension values vector encoding moral foundations, cultural orientation (individualist vs. collectivist), risk appetite, and institutional trust disposition. Calibrated against Schwartz Values Survey datasets across all 14 active markets.

Stratum III
Media & Information Diet

Platform usage patterns, source credibility weighting, filter bubble depth, and susceptibility to framing effects. This layer governs how a Twin processes and weights incoming information — determining which messages land and which are discarded.

Stratum IV
Emotional Reactivity Matrix

A probabilistic model of emotional response to stimuli categories — brand claims, policy announcements, social proof signals, scarcity cues, and authority endorsements. Outputs include valence, arousal, and behavioral intent delta per exposure event.

Stratum V
Social Embedding

Network position, tie strength distribution, opinion leader proximity, and echo chamber coefficient. Governs how influence flows through a simulated population and which nodes act as amplifiers, resistors, or conversion catalysts.

Stratum VI
Longitudinal Memory State

A persistent belief and sentiment ledger that accumulates across simulation time steps. Enables modeling of campaign wear-out, backlash recovery curves, and the compounding effect of consistent messaging on long-term brand positioning.
